Basics of AI Terminology

Artificial Intelligence (AI)
A branch of computer science that enables machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, decision-making, and problem-solving.
Algorithm
A set of rules or steps a computer follows to solve a problem or complete a task.
Machine Learning (ML)
A type of AI where computers learn from data and improve their performance over time without being explicitly programmed.
Data
Information that computers use to learn, such as text, images, or numbers.
Neural Network
A computer system designed to mimic how the human brain processes information, often used in advanced AI tasks like image recognition and natural language understanding.
Deep Learning
A type of machine learning that uses large neural networks with many layers to analyze complex patterns in data.
Natural Language Processing (NLP)
A field of AI focused on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and respond to human language.
Chatbot
A computer program designed to simulate a conversation with a human, often used in customer service or virtual assistants.
Training
The process of teaching an AI system by feeding it data so it can learn to make decisions or predictions.
Model
The result of the AI training process, which is used to make decisions or predictions based on input data.
Prediction
A guess made by an AI system about what will happen or what something means, based on the data it has analyzed.
Bias
When an AI system produces unfair or inaccurate results due to biased data or flawed design.
Automation
The use of AI and other technologies to perform tasks without human involvement.
Ethics in AI
The study of how to use AI responsibly, ensuring it benefits society while avoiding harm.
Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)
A theoretical type of AI that could perform any intellectual task that a human can do. It does not exist yet.
Supervised Learning
A type of machine learning where the AI is trained on labeled data (e.g., pictures of cats labeled as "cat").
Unsupervised Learning
A type of machine learning where the AI tries to find patterns in unlabeled data (e.g., grouping similar images without knowing what they are).
Reinforcement Learning
A type of machine learning where the AI learns by trial and error, receiving rewards or penalties based on its actions.
Turing Test
A test designed to determine if a machine's behavior is indistinguishable from a human's.
Artificial Intelligence Assistant
A virtual helper, like Siri or Alexa, that uses AI to answer questions or perform tasks.
Cloud Computing
Storing and accessing data and AI services over the internet instead of on your computer.
API (Application Programming Interface)
A tool that allows different software programs to communicate with each other, often used to integrate AI capabilities into applications.
Big Data
Extremely large datasets that AI systems use to learn and make predictions.
Robotics
The branch of technology that involves designing, building, and operating robots, often incorporating AI to make robots smarter.
Computer Vision
A field of AI that allows computers to interpret and process visual information from the world, like recognizing faces in photos.
Generative AI
A type of AI that creates new content, such as text, images, or music, often based on patterns in the data it has learned from.
Token
A small piece of text or data that an AI model processes when analyzing or generating language.
Prompt
A question or statement used to guide an AI system's response.
Feedback Loop
The process of improving an AI system by using its results to make adjustments and refine its performance.
Explainability
Making AI systems and their decisions understandable to humans.
